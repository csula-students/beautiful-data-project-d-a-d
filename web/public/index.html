Big Data

Analyzing tweets to Predict the Future of Tech

equalizer

Mongo | ElasticSearch | Kibana

We utilized a NoSQL type database called MongoDB to give us flexability during development in how we store data as well as the ability to easily add new types of data to our database. Mongo's used to store tons and tons of data permanently. By utilizing ElasticSearch we were able to quickly analyze and search our dataset. Using Kibana as a plugin to elasticSearch only made it easier for the team to work on EDA(Exploratory Data Analysis) to find patterns in our data by visualizing the data in

flash_on

Python | Sklearn | NLTK

We found patterns in our raw data quickly thorugh ElasticSearch with Kibana, but to get more accurate results we used python to mine our data. We used NLTK, a python library for natural language machine learning. With NLTK we were able to classify tweets more accurately into what field twitter users were tweeting about and not just by word occurances. We also used Sklearn, a python library for machine learning of all types, to predict the future fields that startups would engage in.

trending_up

HTML | JS | D3

The platform with the highest usage is the platform of choice for how we display our findings. Using web technologies such as HTML, CSS, and Javascript, we are able to convey the results of our project to a wider audience that is available through the internet and in a much prettier way than a typical report. Using D3.js we are able to create completely customizable graphs that are easily readable and scalable in a graphic sense so no browser will pixelate our findings.

The Data

The Data:

Through our analysis, we came up with this chart that best defines our findings of current trends of startup fields. AI and VR are a major factor in this chart with a lot of hype and little public consumption, which can prove to be quite volitile in terms of gaining or losing popularity as there isn't a market yet for Virtual Reality. AI has a large stake in this graph as well, partly due to the fact that our data was gathered during a major world summit for AI and machine learning which is bound to make a splash on social networks such as twitter. Surprisingly, Ecommerce has a major section in startups. This is surprising as there is no hype for ecommerce, but it does make sense as it is much more lucrative as you are dealing directly with money.

The Present

The Present:

Here we are comparing two time intervals. The blue bars represent the first half or older half of our data, while the orange bars represent the newest half of our data. This chart gives us a quick understanding of how each field is trending, whether it is becoming more or less popular. We can see that every field has become less popular, but that could be caused by less events or conferences for each field which will create less impact in tweets and news within each field in the later half of our data.
